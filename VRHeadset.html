Canopy - 3D Rendering of a VR headset

Background

The final project for my materials in design course was to create a Virtual Reality headset in a team of 4. The design and usability catered to our selected user, which was a bed ridden collage student, and was the inspiration for foldable canopy head rest. The materials we chose to create this from would need to be reflected in the rendering with accurate colours and material finishes

Initial Design

The design had been finalized within the group as a foldable headset for easier storage and transportation. Part of the final deliverables were to include a high-quality rendering of the headset on the final poster, and this was, my responsibility. The first renderings were created based on the physical prototype models we had build from lower grade materials and featured the foldable canopy with 4 sections and is attached to the back strap of the headset.

I created the model in Maya by taking top, front and side images of the physical model and modelling to that size in the software, making it as accurate to the real-life prototype as I could. We had planned to use an indigo colour for the straps which would support paper canopy which I had to reflect in the render.

Changes & Ideation

A major change we made to the design was to have the folded canopy attached to the front instead of the back for a more comfortable fit on the head and easier folding when not in use. Making these changes in Maya meant having to move the folded paper to the front, and the bands must also move forward to accommodate the even spaces between the folds.

A solution to this was to create the paper folds with separate planes on the 3D model, so they can be manipulated and positioned easier than a single object having multiple points connected to the headset. This also results in uneven gaps and spaces between the folds, and so separate planes are a better for keeping even spaces.

Final Result

The final renderings were used on display in our final poster during our showcase, in which we presented our final project in the mezzanine on our University Campus.
